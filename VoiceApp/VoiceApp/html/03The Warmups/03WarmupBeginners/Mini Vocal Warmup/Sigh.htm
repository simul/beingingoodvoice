The Sigh

Sighing gently is a great way to release physical tension, and to stimulate blood flow to the vocal folds, literally warming them up.

We sigh:

— when we want something, or when we give up on it,

— because we are annoyed, or because we are pleased.

Always begin to warm up your voice with a few gentle sighs.

A sigh is just air passing through partly closed vocal folds, creating a mixture of friction and vocal sound. But it is also part of our vocal vocabulary. It means something.

Let each sigh mean whatever it means, just as you experience it, and then let it go. There are always plenty more where that one came from.
