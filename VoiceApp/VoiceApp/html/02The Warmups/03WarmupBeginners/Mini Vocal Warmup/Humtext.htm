Continue to warm up your voice by humming gently, and smoothly.

• Release, and drop your jaw,

• allow some breath in silently, easily and quickly,

• bring your lips together gently, with no tensing of lip or facial muscles.

Have that innocent look on your face that says “Who me? I'm not doing anything!”

•Start humming in the middle of your range and slide up and down in pitch, a little higher and a little lower each time.

Do two or three circles, and allow fresh breath in whenever you feel like it.

Remember to allow the breath in silently and quickly.

Catch yourself in the moment of gasping, or reaching for the air, observe the action, and let it go.

The more you notice these things, the more ability you have to choose how you want to do it.

• Think of the sound as going round in a circle, flowing outwards from your centre in a continuous smooth movement.

Be easy, play with the sound. If your voice cracks and wobbles your challenge is to eventually do it smoothly. It takes practice and time for the vocal muscles to become strong and flexible.

There is no need to go as high or as low as you can possibly go, at this stage. This is a warmup, not training.
