The Sigh

Sighing gently is a great way to release physical tension, and to stimulate blood flow to the vocal folds, literally warming them up.

Play the audio, as Flloyd talks you through The Sigh.

We sigh when we want something, or when we give up on it. We sigh because we are annoyed, or because we are pleased. A sigh is just air passing through partly closed vocal folds, creating a mixture of friction and vocal sound. But it is also part of our vocal vocabulary. It means something. Always begin to warm up your voice with a few gentle sighs. Let them mean whatever they mean, just as you experience them. And then, let them go. There's always plenty more where they came from!
